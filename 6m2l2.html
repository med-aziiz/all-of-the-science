Course 6: Earth Science

Module 2: Atmospheric Sciences

Lesson 2.2: Weather Systems & Meteorology

This lesson explains the science of weather—how it is formed, measured, and predicted.

Step 1: What Is Weather?

Definition: Weather refers to short-term atmospheric conditions such as temperature, humidity, precipitation, and wind.

Analogy: Weather is like the daily mood of the sky.

Step 2: Key Components of Weather

  • Temperature: How hot or cold the air is.
  • Humidity: The amount of water vapor in the air.
  • Precipitation: Rain, snow, sleet, or hail falling from the sky.
  • Wind: Movement of air from high-pressure to low-pressure areas.

Step 3: How Weather Systems Form

  • Air Pressure: Differences in pressure cause air to move, creating wind.
  • Fronts: Boundaries between different air masses (e.g., cold fronts and warm fronts).
  • Cyclones & Anticyclones: Low-pressure systems bring stormy weather; high-pressure systems bring clear skies.

Analogy: Like a crowded room where people move from congested areas to less crowded ones.

Step 4: Tools in Meteorology

  • Weather Satellites: Capture images and data of Earth’s atmosphere.
  • Radar Systems: Detect precipitation and storm movement.
  • Weather Stations: Measure local conditions like temperature and wind speed.

Analogy: Just as a doctor uses various instruments to check a patient’s health, meteorologists use multiple tools to “diagnose” the weather.
