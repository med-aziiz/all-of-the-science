Module 2: Reaction Dynamics

Lesson 2.2: Reaction Mechanisms & Kinetics

This lesson explains how reactions occur at the molecular level (mechanisms) and how we quantify reaction speed (kinetics).

Step 1: Reaction Mechanisms

Definition: A reaction mechanism is a detailed, step-by-step description of how reactants convert into products through elementary steps.

Analogy: Like a dance routine where every move leads to the final performance.

Step 2: Understanding Reaction Intermediates

Definition: Intermediates are transient species formed in one step and consumed in another. They help explain the overall reaction pathway.

Step 3: Reaction Kinetics

  • Reaction Rate: The change in concentration per unit time.
  • Factors: Concentration, temperature, and catalysts affect reaction rates.

Step 4: Rate Laws and Activation Energy

  • Rate Law: Rate = k [A]^m [B]^n, where k is the rate constant.
  • Activation Energy: The minimum energy required for a reaction to proceed.
  • Arrhenius Equation: k = A e^(-Ea/RT)

Analogy: Think of activation energy as the height of a hill that must be climbed.
