Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 2: Health & Disease

Lesson 2.3: Pathophysiology & Disease Mechanisms

This lesson examines how normal physiological processes are altered by disease, leading to various symptoms and health conditions.

Step 1: What Is Pathophysiology?

Definition: Pathophysiology studies how normal body functions are disrupted by disease.

Simple Explanation: It’s like understanding what goes wrong in a machine when it malfunctions.

Step 2: Common Disease Mechanisms

  • Infections: Caused by harmful microbes (e.g., bacteria, viruses) that invade the body.
  • Inflammation: The body’s response to injury or infection, often resulting in redness, swelling, and pain.
    Analogy: Like an emergency response to a crisis.
  • Autoimmune Disorders: Conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks the body’s own cells.
    Example: Rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Genetic Disorders: Caused by mutations in genes.
    Example: Cystic fibrosis.

Analogy: Like glitches in a computer program—some errors are caused by external viruses, while others result from bugs in the code.

Step 3: How Diseases Affect the Body

Disease disrupts normal function, leading to symptoms such as pain, fatigue, or fever. Different organs or systems may be affected, which helps in diagnosing and treating the condition.

Analogy: Like a car that starts malfunctioning, triggering warning lights that indicate which part isn’t working.
