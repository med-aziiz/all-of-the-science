Lesson 2.1: Stoichiometry & Balancing Equations

Stoichiometry allows us to quantitatively describe chemical reactions by balancing equations and calculating the amounts of reactants and products.

Step 1: Understanding Chemical Equations

Definition: A chemical equation uses formulas and symbols to represent the reactants and products of a reaction.

Analogy: Think of it like a recipe where the reactants are the ingredients and the products are the final dish.

Step 2: The Need for Balancing Equations

Conservation of Mass: In any chemical reaction, matter is conserved—the number of atoms for each element must be the same on both sides.

Step 3: Balancing Equations – A Detailed Walkthrough

  1. Write the unbalanced equation. Example: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
  2. Count the atoms on each side.
  3. Adjust coefficients until the number of atoms is balanced.
    Example: 2 H₂ + O₂ → 2 H₂O
  4. Verify that all atoms are balanced.

Step 4: Stoichiometry Calculations

  1. Convert grams to moles using molar masses.
  2. Use the mole ratio from the balanced equation to calculate moles of product.
  3. Convert moles back to grams.

Example: 4 g of H₂ (2 g/mol) equals 2 moles, which produces 2 moles of H₂O; then, 2 moles × 18 g/mol = 36 g H₂O.
