Course 4: Chemistry

Module 1: Fundamental Concepts

Lesson 1.1: Atomic Structure & Periodicity

This lesson introduces atoms—the basic units of matter—and shows how their structure leads to periodic trends.

Step 1: What Is an Atom?

Definition: An atom is the smallest unit of an element that retains its chemical properties.

  • Nucleus: The dense central part containing protons (positively charged) and neutrons (neutral).
  • Electron Cloud: The region surrounding the nucleus where electrons (negatively charged) are found.

Analogy: Imagine an atom as a miniature solar system where the nucleus is the sun and electrons are like planets (though, in reality, electrons occupy orbitals, not neat circular orbits).

Step 2: Understanding Electron Arrangement

  • Energy Levels (Shells): Electrons are arranged in layers around the nucleus, with each shell corresponding to a specific energy level.
  • Orbitals: Within each shell, electrons occupy orbitals. Each orbital can hold up to two electrons.

Analogy: Think of energy levels as floors in a building and orbitals as rooms on each floor. Lower floors are filled first before moving upward.

Example: A hydrogen atom has one electron in the first energy level; helium has two electrons filling that level.

Step 3: Periodicity – The Periodic Table

Definition: Periodicity refers to the repeating patterns in the properties of elements as arranged in the periodic table.

  • Atomic Radius: Increases down a group and generally decreases across a period.
  • Ionization Energy: The energy needed to remove an electron; generally increases across a period and decreases down a group.
  • Electronegativity: The ability of an atom to attract electrons; increases across a period and decreases down a group.

Analogy: The periodic table is like a well-organized library where elements with similar "stories" (chemical properties) are grouped together.
