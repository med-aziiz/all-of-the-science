Module 2: Electromagnetism & Waves

Lesson 2.3: Wave Phenomena & Optics

This lesson explores the properties of waves and introduces the fundamentals of optics.

Step 1: Understanding Waves

  • Definition: A disturbance that transfers energy through space without transporting matter.
  • Key Characteristics:
    • Wavelength (λ): Distance between consecutive crests.
    • Frequency (f): Number of cycles per unit time.
    • Speed (v): Calculated by v = f × λ.
    • Amplitude: The height of the wave, related to its energy.

Step 2: Interference and Diffraction

  • Interference: When two waves meet, they can constructively or destructively interfere.
  • Diffraction: Waves bend around obstacles or spread after passing through a narrow opening.
  • Example: The double-slit experiment shows interference patterns of light.

Step 3: Fundamentals of Optics

  • Reflection: Bouncing of waves off a surface (e.g., a mirror).
  • Refraction: Bending of waves as they pass from one medium to another, governed by Snell’s Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂.
  • Lenses and Mirrors: Use reflection and refraction to focus or disperse light.
