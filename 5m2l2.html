Course 5: Astronomy

Module 2: Stellar & Galactic Astronomy

Lesson 2.2: Structure of Galaxies

This lesson explores what galaxies are, the various types of galaxies, and the key components that make up these vast cosmic systems.

Step 1: What Is a Galaxy?

Definition: A galaxy is a massive, gravitationally bound system composed of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter.

Analogy: Imagine a galaxy as a cosmic city, where stars are the buildings and different neighborhoods reflect varying characteristics.

Step 2: Types of Galaxies

  • Spiral Galaxies: Feature a flat, rotating disk with spiral arms (e.g., the Milky Way).
    Analogy: Like a cosmic pinwheel.
  • Elliptical Galaxies: Have an ellipsoidal shape with a smooth brightness distribution (e.g., M87).
    Analogy: A glowing, featureless blob.
  • Irregular Galaxies: Lack a distinct shape, often due to interactions (e.g., the Large Magellanic Cloud).
    Analogy: A city that grew without a clear plan.

Step 3: Galactic Components and Dynamics

  • Disks and Bulges: Disks contain stars, gas, and dust; bulges house older stars.
  • Dark Matter Halo: An unseen component that exerts gravitational influence.
  • Interactions and Mergers: Galaxies interact and merge, shaping their evolution.
