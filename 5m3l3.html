Course 5: Astronomy

Module 3: Cosmology & Extragalactic Studies

Lesson 3.3: Large-Scale Structure of the Cosmos

This lesson explores the cosmic web—the network of galaxy clusters, filaments, and voids that form the large-scale structure of the universe.

Step 1: The Cosmic Web

Definition: The cosmic web is the large-scale network of galaxies and clusters interconnected by filaments, with vast voids in between.

Analogy: Like a three-dimensional spider web with strands representing filaments and the empty spaces as voids.

Step 2: Formation of Structures

  • Gravitational Collapse: Over-dense regions in the early universe collapse under gravity, forming galaxies and clusters.
  • Merging and Growth: Smaller structures merge over time, creating superclusters and extensive filaments.
  • Analogy: Like a lumpy batter in which gravity gathers the denser clumps into “islands” while less dense regions form voids.

Step 3: Voids, Clusters, and Superclusters

  • Voids: Immense regions with very few galaxies.
  • Galaxy Clusters: Groups of galaxies bound together by gravity.
  • Superclusters: Enormous conglomerations of clusters, forming the largest known structures in the universe.
  • Analogy: Like an archipelago where clusters are islands and superclusters form continents amid vast oceans (voids).
