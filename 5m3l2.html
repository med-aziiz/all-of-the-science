Lesson 3.2: Dark Matter & Dark Energy

Although the visible matter in the universe is fascinating, it is only a small fraction of the total mass-energy. This lesson explains the evidence for dark matter and dark energy and their roles in cosmic evolution.

Step 1: Dark Matter – The Invisible Scaffold

  • Definition: Dark matter does not interact with light; its existence is inferred from its gravitational effects.
  • Evidence:
    • Galactic Rotation Curves: Stars orbit faster than expected based on visible mass.
    • Gravitational Lensing: Light bending around galaxies indicates extra mass.
  • Analogy: Like inferring the structure of a building by observing how people move inside it.

Step 2: Dark Energy – The Force Driving Acceleration

  • Definition: Dark energy is a mysterious energy that permeates space and causes the accelerated expansion of the universe.
  • Evidence:
    • Type Ia Supernovae appear dimmer than expected.
    • CMB measurements indicate a high energy density from dark energy.
  • Analogy: Like a gentle but persistent wind that pushes everything apart.

Step 3: The Cosmic Balance

Dark matter and dark energy together dictate the fate of the universe. While dark matter provides the gravitational “glue,” dark energy drives its expansion.
