Lesson 4.2: Cybersecurity Fundamentals

This lesson introduces the basic principles of cybersecurity, including common threats and measures to protect digital systems.

Step 1: What Is Cybersecurity?

Definition: Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access and attacks.

Analogy: Like a home security system with locks, alarms, and cameras.

Step 2: Common Threats

  • Viruses and Malware: Malicious software designed to harm or steal data.
  • Phishing Attacks: Fraudulent attempts to trick you into revealing sensitive information.
  • Ransomware: Malware that locks your data until a ransom is paid.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Overloading a system with traffic to render it unusable.

Step 3: Basic Cybersecurity Measures

  • Encryption: Converting data into a coded format to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Firewalls: Systems that monitor and control network traffic based on security rules.
  • Antivirus Software: Programs that detect and remove malware.
  • Authentication: Methods like passwords and biometrics to verify user identity.

Step 4: Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Regular software updates
  • Data backups
  • User education on safe online practices
  • Secure coding practices

Step 5: Real-World Applications

  • Enterprise Security: Protecting sensitive customer data.
  • Government Defense: Safeguarding critical infrastructure.
  • Everyday Use: Using strong passwords, secure Wi-Fi, and antivirus software.
