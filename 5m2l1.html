Course 5: Astronomy

Module 2: Stellar & Galactic Astronomy

Lesson 2.1: Star Formation & Evolution

This lesson explains how stars are born, evolve on the main sequence, and eventually reach their end stages.

Step 1: The Birth of a Star

  • Molecular Clouds: Vast, cold regions rich in gas and dust (e.g., the Orion Nebula) serve as cosmic nurseries.
    Analogy: A nursery where raw materials are gathered.
  • Gravitational Collapse: Dense clumps within a molecular cloud collapse under gravity, increasing pressure and temperature.
    Analogy: Compressing a sponge until it “lights up” as a protostar.
  • Protostar Stage: A young star forms as the clump continues to collapse and begins nuclear fusion.

Step 2: The Main Sequence and Stellar Evolution

  • Main Sequence: Once fusion ignites, a star enters a long period of stability (e.g., our Sun).
    Analogy: The “adulthood” phase of a star.
  • Mass and Lifespan:
    • High-Mass Stars burn fuel quickly and have shorter lives (e.g., Betelgeuse).
    • Low-Mass Stars burn fuel slowly and live longer (e.g., red dwarfs).

Step 3: The End Stages of Stellar Evolution

  • For Low- and Medium-Mass Stars:
    • They expand into red giants, then shed their outer layers to form planetary nebulae, leaving behind white dwarfs.
  • For High-Mass Stars:
    • They end their lives in supernova explosions, leaving behind neutron stars or black holes.
