Lesson 3.3: Analytical Techniques & Instrumentation

Analytical chemistry focuses on identifying and quantifying the components of substances. This lesson covers both qualitative and quantitative analysis methods along with key instruments.

Step 1: What Is Analytical Chemistry?

Definition: Techniques used to determine the composition and structure of substances.

Importance: Vital for environmental monitoring, pharmaceuticals, forensics, and quality control.

Step 2: Qualitative Analysis

  • Spectroscopy: Interaction of matter with electromagnetic radiation (e.g., IR spectroscopy for functional groups).
  • Chromatography: Separates mixture components (e.g., TLC).

Step 3: Quantitative Analysis

  • Gravimetric Analysis: Measuring mass to determine concentration.
  • Titration: Using a solution of known concentration to determine an unknown concentration.

Step 4: Instrumentation

  • Spectrometers: Measure light intensity across wavelengths.
  • Chromatographs: Separate compounds (e.g., GC, LC).
  • Electrochemical Analyzers: Measure electrical properties such as pH.
