Course 5: Astronomy

Module 1: Introduction to Astronomy

Lesson 1.3: Telescopes & Instrumentation

This lesson covers the evolution and types of telescopes and other instruments that allow us to observe the universe in ever greater detail.

Step 1: The Evolution of Telescopes

  • Historical Development: Early refracting telescopes, as used by Galileo, marked the beginning of detailed astronomical observation.
  • Galileo’s Observations: Revealed the moons of Jupiter and the phases of Venus.
    Analogy: Upgrading from simple binoculars to a high-powered microscope.

Step 2: Types of Telescopes

  1. Refracting Telescopes: Use lenses to bend light; ideal for planets and the Moon.
  2. Reflecting Telescopes: Use mirrors to collect light; suited for distant, faint objects.
    Example: The Hubble Space Telescope.
  3. Radio Telescopes: Detect radio waves; explore parts of the spectrum invisible to optical telescopes.
  4. Space Telescopes: Positioned above Earth’s atmosphere for clear images.

Step 3: Modern Astronomical Instrumentation

  • Spectrographs: Spread light into its spectrum to analyze composition and motion.
  • CCD Cameras: Capture high-resolution digital images of celestial objects.
  • Interferometers: Combine signals from multiple telescopes to simulate a larger telescope.
