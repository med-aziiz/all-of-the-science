Lesson 3.3: Nuclear & Particle Physics

This lesson explores the inner structure of the atom and the fundamental particles that make up matter.

Part 1: Nuclear Physics

  1. Structure of the Atom:
    • Nucleus: Contains protons and neutrons.
    • Electrons: Orbit the nucleus.
    Analogy: A tiny solar system (though much more complex).
  2. Nuclear Forces and Radioactivity:
    • The strong nuclear force holds the nucleus together.
    • Radioactive decay processes include alpha, beta, and gamma decay.
  3. Nuclear Reactions:
    • Fission: Splitting of a heavy nucleus (e.g., in nuclear power plants).
    • Fusion: Combining light nuclei (as in the Sun).

Part 2: Particle Physics

  1. The Standard Model:
    • Quarks: Building blocks of protons and neutrons.
    • Leptons: Includes electrons and neutrinos.
    • Gauge Bosons: Mediate forces (e.g., photons, gluons).
    • Higgs Boson: Associated with the mechanism that gives particles mass.
  2. Interactions and Forces: The four fundamental forces govern particle interactions.
  3. Particle Accelerators: Devices like the LHC reveal properties of fundamental particles.
