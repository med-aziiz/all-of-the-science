Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 1: Biological Foundations

Lesson 1.2: Evolution & Biodiversity

This lesson explains how life changes over time and highlights the vast variety of life forms on Earth.

Step 1: Understanding Evolution

Definition: Evolution is the change in the characteristics of species over generations.

Simple Explanation: Like how fashion trends change over time.

  • Natural Selection: Organisms best suited to their environment tend to survive and reproduce.
    Example: Trees with thicker bark may better survive wildfires.
    Analogy: A contest where only the best-suited traits win.
  • Mutation: Random changes in DNA that can lead to new traits.
    Example: A mutation that changes a flower’s color.
  • Genetic Drift: Random fluctuations in trait frequencies, especially in small populations.

Step 2: The Richness of Life – Biodiversity

Definition: Biodiversity is the variety of life in an ecosystem or on Earth.

Importance: High biodiversity increases ecosystem stability and resilience.

Analogy: Like having many different tools in a toolbox—each plays a vital role.

Example: Tropical rainforests are home to millions of species, each contributing to the ecosystem.

Step 3: Evidence for Evolution

  • Fossil Record: Shows gradual changes in species over millions of years.
    Example: Transitional fossils like Archaeopteryx.
  • Comparative Anatomy: Similar structures in different species indicate common ancestry.
    Example: Bone structure in human arms and bat wings.
  • Molecular Biology: DNA comparisons reveal genetic similarities.

Analogy: Like a family tree built from photographs, heirlooms, and genetic tests.
