Course 5: Astronomy

Module 3: Cosmology & Extragalactic Studies

Lesson 3.1: Big Bang Theory & Universe Expansion

This lesson explains the origin of the universe, its rapid inflation, the evidence provided by the cosmic microwave background (CMB), and the ongoing expansion as observed through redshift.

Step 1: The Origin – From Singularity to Expansion

  • Singularity: A state where density and temperature were extremely high—where all matter and energy were confined in an infinitesimally small point.
    Analogy: All the water in an ocean squeezed into a single drop.
  • Breakdown of Physics: At such extremes, our current laws of physics no longer hold, hinting at new physics.

Step 2: The Rapid Expansion – Inflation

  • Cosmic Inflation: A brief period (around 10⁻³⁶ seconds) when the universe expanded exponentially.
    Analogy: Like rapidly inflating a balloon, spreading out tiny specks of dust.
  • This process smoothed out irregularities and seeded the density fluctuations that later formed galaxies.

Step 3: Evidence of the Big Bang – Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)

  • CMB: The afterglow radiation from the early universe, now observed as a nearly uniform microwave background at ~2.7 K.
    Analogy: The fading embers of a once-roaring fire.
  • Observations of temperature fluctuations in the CMB provide a snapshot of the early universe.

Step 4: Universe Expansion – Hubble’s Law and Redshift

  • Hubble’s Law: v = H₀ × d shows that galaxies recede faster when they are farther away.
    Analogy: Dots on an inflating balloon move apart faster the farther apart they are.
  • Redshift: The stretching of light wavelengths due to cosmic expansion, providing evidence for the universe’s growth.
