Module 3: Advanced Life Sciences

Lesson 3.2: Neuroscience & Behavior

This lesson introduces the basics of neuroscience—the study of the nervous system—and explains how the brain influences behavior.

Step 1: What Is Neuroscience?

Definition: Neuroscience is the study of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves.

Simple Explanation: It’s like exploring the control center of the body that sends and receives signals.

Step 2: Brain Structure and Function

  • Cerebrum: Responsible for thought, memory, and voluntary movement.
  • Cerebellum: Controls balance and coordination.
  • Brainstem: Manages basic life functions (breathing, heart rate).

Analogy: Think of the brain as a computer—each part has a specific role (CPU, graphics card, power supply).

Step 3: How the Brain Influences Behavior

Explanation: Behavior arises from complex interactions between brain activity, environment, and experiences.

Example: Emotional responses such as fear are linked to the amygdala, while the prefrontal cortex helps regulate them.

Analogy: Like a computer program that processes input and produces output (behavior).

Step 4: Modern Techniques in Neuroscience

  • Brain Imaging: Techniques like MRI and fMRI allow us to see brain structure and activity.
  • Electrophysiology: Records electrical signals from neurons (e.g., EEG).

Analogy: Like using high-resolution cameras and microphones to capture a symphony of activity.
