Module 4: Experimental & Applied Physics

Lesson 4.3: Contemporary Research Topics

This lesson offers a brief look at current research areas that are shaping modern physics, including emerging technologies, medical physics, renewable energy, and high-energy physics.

Step 1: Emerging Technologies and New Materials

  • Nanotechnology: Study of materials at the nanoscale for advanced applications.
  • Quantum Computing: Utilizing quantum mechanics to perform computations far beyond classical limits.

Step 2: Advances in Medical Physics

  • Medical Imaging: MRI, PET, and CT scans rely on advanced physics.
  • Radiation Therapy: Precise application of high-energy radiation for cancer treatment.

Step 3: Renewable Energy and Environmental Applications

  • Solar Energy: Research into more efficient photovoltaic cells.
  • Energy Storage: Advances in batteries and other storage technologies.
  • Climate Modeling: Using physics-based models to predict and understand climate change.

Step 4: High-Energy Physics and Cosmology

  • Particle Accelerators: Facilities like the LHC probe the fundamental structure of matter.
  • Dark Matter and Dark Energy: Investigations into mysterious components that shape the universe.
  • Gravitational Waves: New observational windows into cosmic events such as black hole mergers.
