Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 4: Applied Health Sciences

Lesson 4.1: Public Health & Epidemiology

This lesson explains how public health and epidemiology work together to protect communities by preventing and controlling disease.

Step 1: Understanding Public Health

Definition: Public health is the science and practice of preventing disease, prolonging life, and promoting health at the community level.

Simple Explanation: Like a community healthcare team that works to keep everyone healthy.

  • Prevention Programs: Vaccination drives, health education.
  • Health Promotion: Campaigns encouraging exercise, balanced diets, etc.

Step 2: Introduction to Epidemiology

Definition: Epidemiology is the study of how diseases spread and affect populations.

  • Key Concepts:
    • Incidence: New cases in a specific period.
    • Prevalence: Total cases at a given time.
    • Risk Factors: Conditions that increase the likelihood of disease.

Analogy: Like health detectives gathering clues to solve a mystery.

Step 3: How Public Health and Epidemiology Work Together

Data from epidemiological studies guide public health policies and prevention strategies to reduce disease spread.

Analogy: Like a coach using game statistics to develop a winning strategy for a team.
