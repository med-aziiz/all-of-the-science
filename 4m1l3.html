Lesson 1.3: States of Matter & Intermolecular Forces

This lesson examines the different states of matter—solid, liquid, and gas—and the intermolecular forces that determine a substance’s physical properties.

Step 1: States of Matter

  • Solids: Fixed shape and volume; particles are tightly arranged (e.g., ice).
  • Liquids: Fixed volume but no fixed shape; particles flow (e.g., water in a glass).
  • Gases: No fixed shape or volume; particles move freely (e.g., air in a balloon).

Step 2: Intermolecular Forces

  • Van der Waals Forces: Weak attractions due to temporary dipoles.
  • Dipole-Dipole Interactions: Attractions between molecules with permanent dipoles.
  • Hydrogen Bonds: Strong dipole-dipole interactions involving hydrogen bonded to O, N, or F.

Analogy: Like pieces of Velcro that stick together more or less strongly depending on their design.
