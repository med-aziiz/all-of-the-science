Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 4: Applied Health Sciences

Lesson 4.2: Nutrition, Wellness & Lifestyle Medicine

This lesson explores how daily choices—what we eat, how we move, and our overall lifestyle—impact our health. It introduces concepts in nutrition, wellness, and lifestyle medicine.

Step 1: The Importance of Nutrition

Definition: Nutrition is the study of how food provides energy and the building blocks necessary for growth and repair.

Simple Explanation: Food is the fuel for our body.

  • Carbohydrates: Provide energy.
  • Proteins: Build and repair tissues.
  • Fats: Store energy and support cell function.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: Essential for various bodily functions.

Analogy: Like a high-performance car that needs the right mix of fuel and oil to run smoothly.

Step 2: Wellness and Preventive Health

Definition: Wellness is a holistic approach that includes physical, mental, and social well-being, while lifestyle medicine uses healthy habits to prevent and treat disease.

  • Key Practices: Regular exercise, proper sleep, and stress management.

Analogy: Like maintaining a garden—regular watering, sunlight, and care keep it thriving.

Step 3: The Role of Lifestyle Medicine

Definition: Lifestyle medicine focuses on using diet, exercise, and behavioral changes to prevent and treat disease.

Example: The Mediterranean diet helps lower heart disease risk.

Analogy: Like a user manual for your body, teaching you how to stay healthy naturally.
