Lesson 3.2: The Water Cycle & Hydrological Processes

This lesson explains the continuous movement of water through evaporation, condensation, precipitation, runoff, and infiltration.

Step 1: Components of the Water Cycle

  • Evaporation: Liquid water turns to vapor due to heat.
  • Condensation: Water vapor cools to form clouds.
  • Precipitation: Water falls as rain, snow, or hail.
  • Runoff: Water flows over land into rivers and oceans.

Analogy: Like a puddle drying under the sun and then rain refilling it.

Step 2: Groundwater and Infiltration

  • Infiltration: Water soaks into the ground.
  • Groundwater: Water stored in the pores of soil and rock, replenishing wells and springs.
  • Analogy: Like a sponge absorbing water.

Step 3: Importance of the Water Cycle

  • Regulates climate and weather.
  • Supports ecosystems by providing water for life.
