Lesson 3.1: Special & General Relativity

This lesson introduces the revolutionary ideas of relativity that changed our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

Part 1: Special Relativity

  1. The Need for a New Theory: Experiments like Michelson-Morley showed the speed of light is constant, contradicting classical physics.
  2. Fundamental Postulates:
    • Principle of Relativity: The laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames.
    • Constancy of the Speed of Light: Light in a vacuum always travels at ~3×10⁸ m/s, regardless of observer motion.
  3. Time Dilation and Length Contraction:
    • Time Dilation: Δt' = Δt / √(1 - v²/c²) — moving clocks tick slower.
    • Length Contraction: L' = L √(1 - v²/c²) — objects appear shortened in motion.
  4. Mass-Energy Equivalence: The famous equation E = mc² shows mass and energy are interchangeable.

Part 2: General Relativity

  1. Extending to Gravity: General Relativity incorporates acceleration and gravity into the framework.
  2. Gravity as Curvature: Mass and energy curve spacetime. Analogy: A heavy ball on a stretched rubber sheet creates a dip that affects the motion of nearby objects.
  3. Gravitational Time Dilation: Time runs slower in stronger gravitational fields.
  4. Real-World Applications: GPS systems require corrections based on relativity; gravitational lensing is observed in astrophysics.
