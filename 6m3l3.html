Course 6: Earth Science

Module 3: Oceanography & Hydrology

Lesson 3.3: Human Impact on Water Resources

This lesson examines how human activities affect water quality and availability, and discusses sustainable practices to manage water resources.

Step 1: Pollution and Water Quality

  • Point Source Pollution: Pollution from a single, identifiable source (e.g., factory discharge).
  • Non-Point Source Pollution: Diffuse pollution from multiple sources (e.g., agricultural runoff).
  • Analogy: Like a single spill versus multiple small drips that eventually cloud a clear pond.

Step 2: Overuse and Water Scarcity

  • Water Overuse: Excessive water extraction can deplete rivers and aquifers.
  • Impact on Ecosystems: Reduced water levels affect aquatic habitats.
  • Analogy: Like spending too much money from a bank account until the balance runs dangerously low.

Step 3: Climate Change Effects on Water Resources

  • Changing Weather Patterns: More frequent droughts or floods can disrupt the water cycle.
  • Real-World Example: Regions like California experiencing both droughts and intense rainfall events.
  • Analogy: A garden that sometimes is parched and other times flooded.

Step 4: Solutions and Sustainable Practices

  • Water Conservation: Efficient use in households and agriculture (e.g., drip irrigation).
  • Pollution Control: Stricter regulations and cleaner technologies.
  • Restoration Projects: Initiatives to clean and restore water bodies.

Analogy: Like budgeting your money to ensure you always have enough.
