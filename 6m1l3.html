Course 6: Earth Science

Module 1: Geology & Earth's Structure

Lesson 1.3: Geological Hazards – Volcanoes & Earthquakes

This lesson examines natural hazards driven by Earth’s internal dynamics, focusing on how volcanoes form and erupt and how earthquakes occur and impact society.

Step 1: Volcanoes

  • Definition: Volcanoes are openings in the Earth's crust through which molten rock, ash, and gases escape.
  • How They Form: Often form at convergent or divergent plate boundaries.
    Example: Mount St. Helens is known for its explosive eruption in 1980.
    Analogy: Like a pressure cooker releasing built-up steam.

Step 2: Earthquakes

  • Definition: Earthquakes result from a sudden release of energy in the Earth’s crust, causing seismic waves.
  • Causes: Often occur at plate boundaries or along faults.
    Example: The 2011 Tōhoku earthquake in Japan.
    Analogy: Like a bent ruler snapping when pressure becomes too great.

Step 3: Impact and Mitigation

  • Impact: Volcanoes and earthquakes can cause significant damage and loss of life.
  • Mitigation Measures:
    • Building Codes: Structures designed to withstand earthquakes.
    • Monitoring Systems: Seismic networks and volcano observatories help provide early warnings.
