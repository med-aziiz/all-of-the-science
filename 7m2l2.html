Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 2: Health & Disease

Lesson 2.2: Microbiology & Immunology

This lesson explores the world of microbes and explains how the immune system protects us from infections.

Step 1: Introduction to Microbiology

Definition: Microbiology is the study of tiny organisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa.

Simple Explanation: Microbes are everywhere—in the air, water, and on our skin.

Example: Beneficial gut bacteria help digest food, while harmful bacteria can cause infections.

Step 2: The Immune System – Our Body’s Defense

  • Definition: A complex network of cells, tissues, and organs that defends against infections.
  • Key Components:
    • White Blood Cells: Attack and destroy pathogens (e.g., neutrophils, macrophages).
    • Lymphatic System: Transports lymph and filters harmful substances.
    • Antibodies: Proteins that target specific pathogens.
  • Analogy: The immune system is like a security team that identifies and eliminates intruders.

Step 3: How the Immune System Responds to Infection

When a pathogen enters the body, the immune system responds in two phases:

  • Innate Immunity: The immediate, non-specific defense mechanism.
  • Adaptive Immunity: A slower, highly specific response that remembers past infections.

Example: After a flu infection, antibodies are produced so that future encounters are dealt with quickly.
