Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 3: Advanced Life Sciences

Lesson 3.1: Biotechnology & Genetic Engineering

This lesson explores how living systems are used to develop new technologies and how we can directly modify genes to improve or change traits.

Step 1: What Is Biotechnology?

Definition: The application of biological knowledge and techniques to create products and solve problems.

Simple Explanation: Using living cells as tiny factories to produce useful products like medicines and biofuels.

Example: Production of insulin using bacteria.

Step 2: Introduction to Genetic Engineering

Definition: Direct manipulation of an organism’s genetic material.

Simple Explanation: Like editing the recipe book of life to change or improve traits.

  • Recombinant DNA Technology: Combining DNA from different sources.
  • CRISPR-Cas9: A gene-editing tool that makes precise changes in DNA.

Example: Removing a defective gene in a plant to enhance disease resistance.

Analogy: Like using molecular scissors to cut out and replace a faulty sentence in a book.

Step 3: Applications and Ethical Considerations

  • Applications:
    • Medicine: Gene therapies for genetic disorders.
    • Agriculture: Crops with improved nutrition and pest resistance.
    • Industry: Producing enzymes for detergents and biofuels.
  • Ethical Considerations: Safety, environmental impact, and fairness must be considered.
    Example: Debates over GMOs weigh potential benefits against risks.

Analogy: Using genetic engineering responsibly is like handling a powerful kitchen tool—you must follow safety guidelines to ensure beneficial outcomes.
