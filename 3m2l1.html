Lesson 2.1: Fundamentals of Electrostatics & Magnetism

This lesson covers the basics of electric charges, electric fields, and potentials (electrostatics) and introduces magnetic fields and the forces on moving charges (magnetism).

Part 1: Electrostatics

  1. Understanding Electric Charge: Charges come in two types (positive and negative); like charges repel and opposites attract. Analogy: “Social energy” where similar energies repel.
  2. Coulomb’s Law: F = k (q₁ q₂) / r² Example: Two spheres with +2 μC and −2 μC separated by 0.1 m attract each other.
  3. Electric Field (E): Defined as E = F/q, it represents the force per unit charge. Visualization: A “force map” showing how a test charge would be affected.
  4. Electric Potential (V): The work needed per unit charge to move a charge from one point to another.

Part 2: Fundamentals of Magnetism

  1. Magnetic Field (B): Produced by moving charges or changing electric fields, measured in Tesla (T). Example: A bar magnet’s field lines.
  2. Lorentz Force: F = q (v × B) Example: An electron moving perpendicular to a magnetic field follows a circular path.
  3. Unifying Electricity and Magnetism: A changing electric field produces a magnetic field and vice versa.
