Course 7: Life Science and Health

Module 3: Advanced Life Sciences

Lesson 3.3: Ecology & Environmental Health

This lesson examines the interactions between living organisms and their environment, and how these interactions impact human health.

Step 1: What Is Ecology?

Definition: Ecology is the study of the relationships between living organisms and their environment.

Simple Explanation: How plants, animals, and other organisms interact with each other and their surroundings.

Step 2: Environmental Health

Definition: Environmental health examines how environmental factors (air, water, soil) influence human health.

Analogy: Like a doctor checking vital signs to ensure a system is functioning properly.

Step 3: The Interconnection Between Ecology and Human Health

Healthy ecosystems provide clean air, water, and food; when these systems are disrupted, human health is affected.

Example: Wetlands filter pollutants and protect against floods.

Analogy: Like a well-organized household where every system works together to keep residents healthy.
