Module 4: Applied Chemistry

Lesson 4.3: Environmental & Industrial Chemistry

This lesson explores how chemical principles are applied to solve real-world problems in energy, pollution control, and manufacturing.

Step 1: Environmental Chemistry

Definition: The study of chemical processes in the environment, including the fate and transport of chemicals in air, water, and soil.

Example: Designing biodegradable plastics to reduce long-term pollution.

Step 2: Industrial Chemistry

Definition: Application of chemical processes to manufacture products on a large scale.

  • Catalysis: Using catalysts to increase efficiency.
  • Process Optimization: Maximizing yield while minimizing waste.
  • Example: The Haber Process for ammonia synthesis.

Step 3: Integration and Sustainability

  • Sustainability: Designing processes that use renewable resources and minimize environmental impact.
  • Green Chemistry: Principles aimed at reducing hazardous substances.

Example: Using water instead of toxic solvents in pharmaceutical manufacturing.
