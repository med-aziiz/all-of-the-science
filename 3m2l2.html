Module 2: Electromagnetism & Waves

Lesson 2.2: Maxwell’s Equations & Electrodynamics

Maxwell’s Equations are the four fundamental equations that describe how electric and magnetic fields are generated and altered. This lesson also covers electrodynamics—how these fields vary with time.

Step 1: Introducing Maxwell’s Equations

  1. Gauss’s Law for Electricity: ∇⋅E = ρ/ε₀ States that the total electric flux is proportional to the enclosed charge.
  2. Gauss’s Law for Magnetism: ∇⋅B = 0 Indicates that there are no magnetic monopoles (magnetic field lines form closed loops).
  3. Faraday’s Law of Induction: ∇×E = -∂B/∂t A changing magnetic field induces an electric field.
  4. Ampère-Maxwell Law: ∇×B = μ₀J + μ₀ε₀ ∂E/∂t Electric currents and changing electric fields produce magnetic fields.

Step 2: Electrodynamics in Action

  • Time-Varying Fields: For example, alternating current (AC) produces a continuously changing magnetic field.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Changing electric and magnetic fields propagate as waves (radio waves, visible light, etc.). Analogy: Like ripples on a pond.

Step 3: Practical Applications

  • Wireless Communication
  • Medical Imaging (e.g., MRI)
  • Everyday Electronics (e.g., transformers, induction cooktops)
