Lesson 1.3: Anatomy & Physiology Overview

This lesson provides an overview of anatomy (the structure of organisms) and physiology (how these structures function), explaining how living organisms are built and how they operate.

Step 1: What Is Anatomy?

Definition: Anatomy is the study of the parts and structures of living organisms.

Simple Explanation: Like learning the parts of a car.

  • Gross Anatomy: Study of large structures (organs, organ systems).
  • Microscopic Anatomy: Study of cells and tissues using a microscope.

Step 2: What Is Physiology?

Definition: Physiology is the study of how the parts of an organism work together to support life.

Simple Explanation: Like understanding how a carâ€™s engine, fuel system, and electronics work together.

  • Key Processes: Circulation, respiration, digestion, etc.

Step 3: Integration of Anatomy and Physiology

Knowing the structure (anatomy) helps us understand function (physiology).

Detailed Example: The structure of the lungs (with branching airways and alveoli) maximizes the surface area for gas exchange.

Analogy: Like a sponge whose porous structure allows it to absorb water efficiently.
