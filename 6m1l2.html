Course 6: Earth Science

Module 1: Geology & Earth's Structure

Lesson 1.2: Rock Cycle, Minerals & Fossils

This lesson explains the continuous process of rock transformation (the rock cycle), the formation of minerals, and how fossils record Earth’s history.

Step 1: The Rock Cycle

Definition: The rock cycle describes how rocks are formed, weathered, eroded, and reformed.

  1. Igneous Rocks: Form from cooled and solidified magma (e.g., granite).
  2. Sedimentary Rocks: Form by the accumulation and cementation of sediments (e.g., sandstone).
  3. Metamorphic Rocks: Form when existing rocks are transformed by heat and pressure (e.g., marble from limestone).

Analogy: Think of the rock cycle as recycling paper—old paper is transformed into new paper.

Step 2: Minerals

Definition: Minerals are naturally occurring, inorganic solids with a specific chemical composition and crystalline structure.

Example: Quartz (SiO₂) is one of the most common minerals.

Analogy: Imagine rock candy forming as sugar crystallizes from a solution.

Step 3: Fossils

Definition: Fossils are the preserved remains or traces of ancient life, typically found in sedimentary rocks.

  • Body Fossils: Actual remains like bones or shells.
  • Trace Fossils: Evidence of past life, such as footprints or burrows.

Analogy: Fossils are like time capsules that capture a moment in Earth’s history.
