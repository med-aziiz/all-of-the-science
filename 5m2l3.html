Module 2: Stellar & Galactic Astronomy

Lesson 2.3: Interstellar Medium & Nebulae

This lesson explores the interstellar medium (ISM)—the gas and dust between stars—and the various types of nebulae that are both stellar nurseries and remnants.

Step 1: What Is the Interstellar Medium?

Definition: The ISM is the matter (gas, dust, cosmic rays) that fills the space between stars in a galaxy.

Analogy: Think of the ISM as the “atmosphere” of a galaxy.

Step 2: Nebulae – Stellar Nurseries and Remnants

  • Emission Nebulae: Glow due to ionized gas (e.g., Orion Nebula).
  • Reflection Nebulae: Shine by reflecting starlight (e.g., around the Pleiades).
  • Dark Nebulae: Dense clouds that block light (e.g., Horsehead Nebula).

Step 3: Role of the ISM and Nebulae in Star Formation

Regions in the ISM that are dense and cold can collapse under gravity to form new stars. The recycling of material from dying stars enriches the ISM for future generations.
