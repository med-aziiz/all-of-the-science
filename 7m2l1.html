Lesson 2.1: Human Physiology in Depth

This lesson examines the major body systems and explains how they work together to keep us healthy.

Step 1: Overview of Body Systems

Definition: The human body is made up of many systems (groups of organs and tissues) that perform specific functions.

  • Circulatory System: Transports blood, oxygen, and nutrients.
  • Respiratory System: Exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide.
  • Digestive System: Breaks down food for energy.
  • Nervous System: Controls body activities through electrical signals.
  • Musculoskeletal System: Provides structure and enables movement.

Step 2: The Circulatory System

What It Does: Delivers oxygen and nutrients to tissues and removes waste products.

Detailed Example: The heart pumps oxygen-rich blood from the lungs through arteries, and deoxygenated blood returns via veins.

Step 3: The Respiratory System

What It Does: Enables breathing and gas exchange between the body and the environment.

Detailed Explanation: Air enters through the nose/mouth, travels down the trachea, reaches the lungs, and in the alveoli oxygen enters the blood while carbon dioxide is expelled.

Analogy: Like an air filter that supplies fresh air and removes used air.

Step 4: Coordination Between Systems

All body systems work together seamlessly. For example, the respiratory system supplies oxygen to the blood, which the circulatory system then distributes to tissues.

Analogy: Like a well-organized team where each player has a specific role.
