Course 6: Earth Science

Module 2: Atmospheric Sciences

Lesson 2.3: Climate Change & Environmental Impact

This lesson explores long-term changes in the Earth’s climate, the greenhouse effect, and the effects of human activity on our environment.

Step 1: Understanding Climate vs. Weather

Definition: Weather refers to short-term atmospheric conditions, while climate is the average of these conditions over a long period (typically 30 years or more).

Analogy: Weather is like your daily mood, while climate is your overall personality.

Step 2: The Greenhouse Effect

Definition: A natural process where greenhouse gases trap heat, warming the Earth.

  • Key Gases: CO₂, methane, and water vapor.
  • Analogy: Like a blanket that keeps you warm at night.

Step 3: Causes and Effects of Climate Change

  • Human Activities: Burning fossil fuels and deforestation increase greenhouse gas levels.
  • Natural Factors: Volcanic eruptions and solar variations also affect climate.
  • Effects: Rising temperatures, melting ice, and more extreme weather events.

Analogy: Like a garden that sometimes gets too little water and other times is flooded.

Step 4: Mitigation and Adaptation

  • Mitigation: Reducing greenhouse gas emissions by using renewable energy and increasing efficiency.
  • Adaptation: Adjusting to changes with measures like flood defenses and drought-resistant crops.

Analogy: Mitigation is like fixing a leaky roof before the rain; adaptation is like wearing a raincoat when it rains.
