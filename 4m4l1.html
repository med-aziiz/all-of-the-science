Lesson 4.1: Biochemistry & Molecular Biology

This lesson bridges chemistry and biology by exploring biomolecules and the molecular processes within living organisms.

Step 1: Introduction to Biomolecules

Definition: Biomolecules are the chemical components of living things including proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, and lipids.

  • Proteins: Made of amino acids; perform functions like catalysis and structure.
  • Nucleic Acids: DNA and RNA carry genetic information.
  • Carbohydrates: Provide energy and structural support.
  • Lipids: Store energy and form cell membranes.

Analogy: Think of a cell as a factory: DNA is the blueprint, RNA the messenger, proteins the machines, and lipids the walls.

Step 2: The Structure and Function of DNA

  • Components: Sugar (deoxyribose), phosphate group, and nitrogenous bases (A, T, C, G).
  • Double Helix: Two strands twist together; base pairing (A-T and C-G) ensures accurate replication.

Analogy: DNA is like a twisted ladder where the sides are the sugar-phosphate backbones and the rungs are the base pairs.

Step 3: Proteins and Enzymes

  • Proteins: Polymers of amino acids that fold into specific shapes to perform functions.
  • Enzymes: Proteins that catalyze chemical reactions by lowering activation energy.

Example: Amylase in saliva breaks down starches.

Step 4: Techniques in Molecular Biology

  • PCR: Amplifies small DNA segments.
  • Gel Electrophoresis: Separates DNA fragments by size.
