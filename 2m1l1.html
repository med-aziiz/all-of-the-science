Course 2: Computer Science

Module 1: Introduction to Computing

Lesson 1.1: History & Evolution of Computers

This lesson takes you on a journey through the evolution of computers—from early calculation tools to modern electronic machines.

Step 1: Early Beginnings

The Abacus and Early Calculators:

  • What Is an Abacus?: A simple device with beads on rods used for calculation.
  • Example: Imagine using beads to track your daily expenses.
  • Significance: Laid the groundwork for systematic computation.

Step 2: Mechanical Calculators

During the 17th and 18th centuries, inventors such as Blaise Pascal and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz developed mechanical calculators that used gears and levers.

  • How They Worked: Numbers were input via dials or levers, and gears performed arithmetic operations.
  • Analogy: Think of it as an early “mechanical smartphone” that only did math.

Step 3: The Dawn of Modern Computers

The Birth of Electronic Computers:

  • World War II and Beyond: Development of machines like the ENIAC to perform complex calculations.
  • Key Figures: Alan Turing (theoretical computation) and innovations such as vacuum tubes.
  • Significance: Marked the shift from mechanical to electronic computation.

Step 4: Personal Computers and the Internet Era

  • Personal Computers: The advent of microprocessors in the 1970s made computers accessible to individuals.
  • The Internet: Transformed computers into interconnected devices for global communication.
