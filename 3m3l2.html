Lesson 3.2: Quantum Mechanics Principles

Quantum Mechanics deals with the behavior of particles at the smallest scales, where classical physics no longer applies.

Step 1: The Birth of Quantum Mechanics

Classical physics couldn’t explain phenomena like blackbody radiation or the photoelectric effect. Pioneers like Planck and Einstein introduced quantization.

Step 2: Wave-Particle Duality

Definition: Particles exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties. Example: Electrons create an interference pattern in the double-slit experiment.

Step 3: The Uncertainty Principle

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: Δx · Δp ≥ ħ/2 It is impossible to know both the position and momentum of a particle exactly at the same time.

Step 4: Quantization and Energy Levels

Energy in an atom is quantized; electrons occupy specific energy levels and jump between them when absorbing or emitting energy.

Step 5: The Role of the Wave Function

The wave function describes the probability of finding a particle in a particular state. Rather than definite positions, quantum mechanics offers probabilities.
