Lesson 2.4: The Internet – What It Is and How It Works

This lesson explains the structure of the Internet—from nodes and routers to protocols and DNS—detailing how global connectivity is achieved.

What Is the Internet?

Definition: A global network of interconnected computers communicating via standardized protocols.

Analogy: A vast network of roads connecting cities (computers) through data highways.

How the Internet Works: A Step-by-Step Explanation

  1. Networking Basics:
    • Nodes and Routers: Devices (nodes) and routers that direct data traffic.
    • Example: An email travels from your computer through various routers.
  2. Protocols: The Rules of Communication
    • TCP/IP: Ensures reliable data transmission.
    • HTTP/HTTPS: Protocols for transferring web content, with HTTPS adding security.
  3. Data Transmission:
    • Packets: Data is split into small packets.
    • Routing: Routers forward packets along the best path; packets are reassembled at the destination.
  4. Domain Name System (DNS):
    • Translates human-friendly domain names into IP addresses.
