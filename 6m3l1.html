Module 3: Oceanography & Hydrology

Lesson 3.1: Ocean Currents & Marine Geology

This lesson explores how ocean currents move and how the seafloor's structure affects marine environments.

Step 1: Understanding Ocean Currents

  • Definition: Ocean currents are continuous, directed movements of seawater.
  • Key Drivers:
    • Wind: Drives surface currents.
    • Earth’s Rotation: The Coriolis effect curves currents.
    • Temperature & Salinity: Create density differences that drive deep currents.
  • Example: The Gulf Stream transports warm water from the Gulf of Mexico to Europe.
  • Analogy: Like rivers within the ocean that flow and carry heat and nutrients.

Step 2: Marine Geology – The Seafloor and Its Features

  • Mid-Ocean Ridges: Underwater mountain ranges where new crust forms (e.g., the Mid-Atlantic Ridge).
  • Ocean Trenches: Deep valleys formed at convergent plate boundaries (e.g., the Mariana Trench).
  • Analogy: Like mountains and valleys on land, but under water.

Step 3: Interplay Between Currents and Geology

The shape of the seafloor influences how currents flow, steering water along ridges and through trenches.

Analogy: Like water flowing through a garden with rocks and hills—the terrain directs the flow.
