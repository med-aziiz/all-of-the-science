Course 4: Chemistry

Module 2: Reaction Dynamics

Lesson 2.3: Chemical Thermodynamics

This lesson examines the energy changes during chemical reactions, focusing on enthalpy, Gibbs free energy, and entropy.

Step 1: Energy Changes in Reactions

  • Enthalpy (H): Total heat content at constant pressure.
  • Exothermic Reactions: Release heat (ΔH < 0).
  • Endothermic Reactions: Absorb heat (ΔH > 0).

Step 2: Spontaneity and Gibbs Free Energy

Gibbs Free Energy (G): ΔG = ΔH - TΔS

  • If ΔG < 0, the reaction is spontaneous.
  • If ΔG > 0, the reaction is non-spontaneous.
  • If ΔG = 0, the system is in equilibrium.

Step 3: Entropy and Disorder

Entropy (S): A measure of disorder; in most natural processes, entropy increases.

Analogy: Like a tidy room gradually becoming messy over time.

Step 4: Equilibrium and Le Chatelier’s Principle

  • Chemical Equilibrium: When forward and reverse reaction rates are equal.
  • Le Chatelier’s Principle: If a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it will shift to counteract the change.
