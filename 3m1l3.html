Module 1: Classical Mechanics

Lesson 1.3: Rotational Motion & Gravitation

This lesson extends our study of motion to include rotation and the gravitational forces that govern both everyday objects and celestial bodies.

Step 1: Rotational Motion

  • Angular Position (θ): The angle relative to a reference line.
  • Angular Velocity (ω): The rate of change of angular position; ω = Δθ/Δt.
  • Angular Acceleration (α): The rate of change of angular velocity; α = Δω/Δt.
  • Moment of Inertia (I): A measure of an object’s resistance to rotational change. Analogy: Like mass in linear motion.
  • Rotational Kinetic Energy: KE_rot = ½ I ω².

Step 2: Gravitation

  • Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation: F = G (m₁ m₂) / r² where G is the gravitational constant.
  • Gravitational Potential Energy: PE = -G (m₁ m₂) / r (the negative sign indicates work must be done to separate the masses).
